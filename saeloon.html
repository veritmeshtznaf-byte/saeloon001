From 2020–2022, a prolonged dispute occurred between Kahk and Mazen Mohamed (Port Said). Key points:

  • In 2020 Kahk was in his "memer prime" creating memes on a new Facebook profile.
  • Kahk became friends with Mazen Mohamed, a prankster with technical knowledge. Together they created "INFO • SCP".
  • The group grew to ~300 members in one month, but Mazen engaged in pranks and social engineering.
  • After ~1 year, the group's administration pages were banned on Facebook due to false reports, allegedly by Mazen. Kahk blocked him.
  • Mazen allegedly burned Kahk's phone motherboard; Kahk only realized months later when accessing his account from another device.
  • Kahk then created a new Facebook account "Zuck Bug" with enhanced security. Mazen eventually lost interest, allowing Kahk to continue Saeloon.

Influences & Internal Change

Internet culture and alternative media influenced the group, including the work of Jack Stauber. Personal changes, such as Saged Khaled's religious reorientation, affected group dynamics. Some communications moved to private chats like "Sanitarium".

Dissolution

On 5 November 2024, Kahk announced the closure of Saeloon public chats. Final message:

Arabic: "@الكل هذا سيكون كافي ايها الرفاق ويا شعب السايلون العظيم يجب ان نرحل الان ولكن السايلون سيبقى في قلوبنا للابد، أستودعكم الله الذي لا تضيع ودائعَه"
English: "@Everyone, this will be enough, comrades, and to the great people of Saeloon. We must leave now, but Saeloon will remain in our hearts forever. I entrust you to God, whose trust is never lost."

Some members continued limited private interactions in "Sanitarium".

Analysis

Saeloon is an example of symbolic micronationalism online: fictional statehood and shared symbols strengthened in-group cohesion and identity.

Legacy

Though public activity ended, Saeloon continues in memory, archives, and copied materials. It is primarily an archival artifact rather than a living online community.
